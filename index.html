Forest Service Interactive Map

Did you know that, just like the population census, the US conducts a yearly census on forests? The Forest Inventory and Analysis (FIA) program collects data on "how much forest exists, where it exists, who owns it, how it is changing, how the trees and other forest vegetation are growing, how much has died or been removed, and how the harvested trees have been used in recent years." (USDA, 2016)
Select an attribute from the dropdown menu to see how several US states measure up.
The attributes represented are: acres of forested area; acres of land used for timber; number (in hundreds) of live trees; number (in hundreds) of dead trees; and metric tons of carbon stored in live trees. All data have been normalized by area.

Data sourced from https://www.fia.fs.fed.us/.
